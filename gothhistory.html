The History of the Goth Subculture:

Goths are usually mischaracterized as evil, satan worshippers that are always miserable and full of woe, but I’m here to negate those misconceptions and teach you about the true history of goths. Together, we will be looking at the history, music, and fashion of the Goth subculture and how it’s changed into what it is today.

Firstly, let’s begin with a brief history of what goth is. The history of “Goth” is a very complex history, it spans over generations and is deeply rooted in political activism and counter-culture. The term “gothic rock” was first used to describe the music of the Doors in 1967. According to Claire Nally from the Museum of Youth Culture, “Goth developed from various other youth subcultures, including punk.” Being goth is seen as a “resistance of the status quo” and is fundamentally anti-consumerism and anti-racism.
